Quick definitions

In-the-money (ITM): An option with intrinsic value. A call is ITM when strike < stock price; a put is ITM when strike > stock price.

Out-of-the-money (OTM): An option with no intrinsic value. A call is OTM when strike > stock price; a put is OTM when strike < stock price.

Premium: The price paid (buy) or received (sell) for the option contract.

Break-even: The stock price where profit/loss equals zero at expiration after accounting for premium.
